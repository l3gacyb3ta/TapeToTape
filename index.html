What is this

Code your music. Ship it to the world.

Tape to Tape is a program for teen hackers who want to make something that sounds as good as it runs. Using Sonic Pi — a live coding environment built for beginners — you'll write code that plays music. When you're done, your track goes on a real Bandcamp compilation album alongside everyone else who shipped.

No music theory required. No expensive software. Just a browser, some curiosity, and a willingness to hit play and see what happens.

— ✦ —

How it works

Three steps to the album.

01
Open the editor Use the Tape to Tape Sonic Pi editor — no install needed. It runs right in your browser.
02
Write a song Spend at least an hour making something. It doesn't have to be good. It has to be yours. Export it as a WAV when you're done.
03
Submit it Fill out the submission form with your WAV file and your Sonic Pi code. We'll review it and add it to the album.
— ✦ —

The prize

Your name on a real record.

Every accepted track gets added to the Tape to Tape Hack Club compilation on Bandcamp. Your name in the credits. Your music on a page people can actually visit. A permanent artifact of something you made.

— ✦ —

Rules

Keep it real.

  • Your track must be made in the Tape to Tape Sonic Pi editor.
  • Minimum one hour of work. Track your time honestly.
  • Submit your .wav export and your Sonic Pi source code.
  • Original compositions only. Don't just copy a tutorial verbatim.
  • Open source: your code goes in a public GitHub repo.
  • You must be a teenager (18 or younger) to participate.
